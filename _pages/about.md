I'm a PhD student at UCL's [Spiers Lab](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/bartlett/news/2024/sep/spiers-lab), where I study spatial navigation and environmental neuroscience — in plain terms: how does the built environment shape the brain?

My background spans human geography (Université de Poitiers), cognitive neuroscience (Universiteit van Amsterdam), and data science (Le Wagon). I've studied macaques in Malaysian rainforests, fine-tuned LLMs at [INSERM](https://www.inserm.fr/en/), built hydrogen energy models for the [ANR](https://anr.fr/Project-ANR-20-CE05-0035/) in French Polynesia, and taught Python to corporate teams at Michelin, Stellantis, and ABB.

I speak French and English natively, Spanish (B2), and am working on German and Portuguese.
