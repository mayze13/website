I'm a PhD student at UCL's [Spiers Lab](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/bartlett/news/2024/sep/spiers-lab), where I study the impact of environmental design on cognitive function and well-being — in plain terms: how do cities affect our brains?

My background spans human geography (Université de Poitiers), cognitive neuroscience (Universiteit van Amsterdam), and data science (Le Wagon). I've spent time studying macaques in Malaysian rainforests, fine-tuning LLMs at [INSERM](https://www.inserm.fr/en/), building hydrogen energy models for the [ANR](https://anr.fr/Project-ANR-20-CE05-0035/) in French Polynesia, and teaching Python to corporate teams at companies like Michelin, Stellantis, and ABB.

I'm interested in neurourbanism, spatial cognition, AI for good, and the occasional jazz standard. I speak French and English natively, Spanish professionally, and am working on German and Portuguese.
