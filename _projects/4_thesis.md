A critical literature review written as part of my Master's in Brain and Cognitive Sciences at the Universiteit van Amsterdam (graded 9/10). The review examines the empirical and theoretical literature on why humans have a strong aesthetic preference for natural environments, and what restorative effects these environments may have on cognition and well-being.

**Key themes explored:**
- Attention Restoration Theory (ART) and Stress Recovery Theory (SRT)
- Evolutionary accounts of nature preference (biophilia, savanna hypothesis)
- Neural correlates of aesthetic experience in natural vs. urban settings
- Methodological critiques of restoration research
- Implications for urban design and green space policy

This work directly informs my current PhD research at UCL's Spiers Lab, where I study how environmental design affects cognitive function and well-being in urban contexts.
